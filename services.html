Model

At NDLAB, we offer an outsourcing model that involves delegating a portion or the entirety of your project to our expert team. This approach allows you to entrust us with specific tasks, entire projects, or distinct modules, leveraging our expertise to achieve your goals.

Benefits of Outsourcing with NDLAB:

  • Rapid task completion due to our efficient workflow.
  • Reduction in product development costs.
  • Collaboration with a cohesive team of seasoned developers.
  • Flexible project management methodologies tailored to your needs.

Ideal for:

  • Startups seeking expert IT assistance.
  • Product companies looking to augment their development capabilities.
  • IT companies in need of specialized skills or additional manpower.

In our outstaffing model, NDLAB places our skilled personnel under your direction to meet specific challenges. These professionals remain part of our team but dedicate their full-time efforts to your project. You manage their daily tasks, while we ensure the quality and adherence to the agreed timelines.

Benefits of Outstaffing with NDLAB:

  • Immediate development support for your existing team.
  • Ability to schedule and plan the developer's time effectively.
  • Cost savings with flexible engagement terms, such as part-time or fixed-duration involvement.

Ideal for:

  • Production companies require additional IT expertise.
  • Businesses with limited in-house IT staff or without a dedicated IT team.
  • Medium to large IT companies need specialized personnel for specific projects or timeframes.

Product

The Fixed Price model at NDLAB is ideal for projects with clearly defined requirements and timelines. We assess the total scope of work and offer a fixed cost for the entire project. This model provides complete predictability of expenses and deadlines, making it an excellent choice for projects with a limited budget or strict timelines.
At NDLAB, we offer a Time & Material payment model, perfectly suited for projects where requirements may change or are not fully defined from the start. This approach allows our clients to flexibly manage the development, making adjustments at any stage of the process. Payment is calculated based on the actual time and resources spent, ensuring transparency and flexibility in budget management.
For long-term collaboration and ongoing support, NDLAB offers a retainer model. This means that clients pay a regular, predetermined fee for a certain volume of work or for the constant availability of our team. This model is perfect for companies that require continuous technical support, development, and maintenance.
NDLAB also offers a flexible hybrid model, combining elements of different payment models. For example, we can use a fixed price for certain stages of the project and switch to a Time & Material basis for other parts that require more flexibility. This model is ideal for projects that combine well-defined tasks with elements that need adaptability in development.

Methodology

Scrum, a subset of Agile, focuses on team collaboration and the timely delivery of software products. It involves roles like Scrum Master and Product Owner, and ceremonies such as daily stand-ups, sprint planning, and retrospectives. Scrum is ideal for projects requiring rapid iterations and adaptability to changing requirements.
Kanban, another Agile framework used at NDLAB, emphasizes continuous delivery and workflow visualization. Using a Kanban board, we manage and track work in progress, ensuring efficiency and flexibility. This approach is perfect for projects that require ongoing maintenance or have less defined schedules.
Our Incremental approach is ideal for phased product development, involving multiple launch phases and the gradual building of the final product. At NDLAB, we start by releasing a product with basic functionality and then progressively add new features. This methodology allows for flexibility in adding functionalities and adapting to feedback, culminating in a fully developed system.
NDLAB also utilizes the Waterfall methodology for projects with well-defined requirements and a clear scope. This methodology follows a sequential approach, where each phase of development must be completed before moving on to the next. It begins with requirement gathering, followed by design, development, and testing. Waterfall is known for its straightforward project management, fixed costs, and preset timelines. It is best suited for projects where the scope and requirements are fixed and unlikely to change.
